Battery Backup:

We've been stranded in some manner or fashion by the folks at Eskom The truth is that load-shedding is going to be around, indefinitely and intermittently in the near future. What are you doing to ensure that you don't become trapped inside the garage or out? One option, which is an essential part of any automated door installation is to install an emergency release mechanism in place. The emergency release mechanism is inserted inside the curtain of the door with an opening for the key facing towards the outside. A steel cable is connected to the motor's safety release valve. When the power is cut off then you'll have put your keys into the device and give it a twist then pull and it will draw the steel cable to let the door go to the motor. While emergency release mechanisms are extremely effective, they require you to leave the safety of your home or vehicle to open the door. Then, you'll need physically raise the door manually. This could be a problem when the door is extremely heavy and isn't keen to remain in an closed position. You'll need to find someone else to keep the door open as you move your vehicle.


The other option is to use an alternative battery backup system to power the garage door's motor. This will allow you to remain comfortably and safe inside your home or car and still be able to access your garage in the event of power failures. Similar to gate motors, you garage's door motor has to switch automatically between 24v and 220v and also be able to charge batteries when 220v is turned on. The problem is that there aren't all motors designed to run on 24v and all motors from the past are in this category. Motors such as such as the Digi 2, Gemini, Brano, Alladin, or the Pro Alpha 2000 are all 220v motors and are thus not compatible with batteries that run 24v. Advanced UPS systems that make use of inverters may be used for these motors, however the costs of these systems is too costly to justify moving in this route. Tip Up Doors:


Tip-up doors function in a way that's slightly difficult to install motors. The motor shaft has to be mounted at an angle and sloping toward the ground. This permits the natural motion of the door as it opens and closing it, and guarantees an effortless operation. A few of the older tip up doors are operated using counter-weight mechanisms, rather than the modern spring system. These doors that have counter-weights cannot be controlled, since the door has to be pulled or pushed at various angles during its operation. However, it is possible to change the door with spring mechanisms before automating the door like a normal one.
